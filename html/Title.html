Gluten is a general name for the proteins found in wheat , barley rye etc...
A cross between wheat and rye , Gluten acts as a biologocial adhesive to hold the shape of food together.
Gluten can be found in a variety of foods, but mostly in foods found under the term carbohydrate heavy such as pizza , pasta, yoghurt and bread.
Common foods that regularly contain ingredients with gluten include: pastas, breads, crackers, seasonings and spice mixes.
Gluten can also be found in unexpected places such as fish and chip shops where the chips are cooked in the same oil used to cook the battered foods.
It can also be found in most Chinese Takeaways where wheat is used to thicken up the sauces.
Gluten may also be present in other foods such as confectionery, sausages and smallgoods, sauces, dressings and other condiments, canned soups,
stock cubes, malted and cereal drinks, yeast extract spreads, soy milk, custard powder, icing sugar mixture,
baking powder, beer, medications, etc...
